# Callum Beattie Fan Club — Design Brainstorm

## Context
Fan club website for Scottish indie singer-songwriter Callum Beattie. Goal: maximise follower growth for both the fan club and Callum. Audience: passionate Scottish music fans, 20s–40s. Tone: raw, authentic, energetic, community-driven.

---

<response>
<text>
**Idea 1: "Glasgow Brutalist Punk"**

- **Design Movement**: Post-punk brutalism meets Scottish gig-poster aesthetic
- **Core Principles**: Raw energy, bold typography, high-contrast monochrome with electric accent, grid-breaking asymmetry
- **Color Philosophy**: Near-black backgrounds (#0d0d0d), stark white text, single electric accent (neon lime #b5ff3a) used sparingly for CTAs and highlights. Evokes the grit of Glasgow's music scene.
- **Layout Paradigm**: Staggered, offset grid — sections break out of their containers, text overlaps imagery, content bleeds to edges. No centered layouts.
- **Signature Elements**: Large condensed uppercase type bleeding off edges; torn-paper dividers between sections; hand-drawn underlines on key phrases
- **Interaction Philosophy**: Hover states feel like a spotlight hitting the stage — fast, sharp, no easing
- **Animation**: Snap-in entrances (no easing), text glitch effects on hover, parallax on hero image
- **Typography System**: Bebas Neue (display/headings) + Source Serif 4 (body) — industrial meets readable
</text>

**Idea 2: "Scottish Anthemic — Stadium Rock Warmth"**

- **Core Principles**: Emotional warmth, community feeling, photography-forward, generous whitespace with bold type moments
- **Color Philosophy**: Deep charcoal (#1a1a1a) background with warm amber/gold (#f5a623) as the primary accent — evoking stage lighting and Scottish autumn. Off-white (#f0ede8) for text areas.
- **Layout Paradigm**: Full-bleed photography sections alternating with tight editorial text columns. Asymmetric two-column layouts. Hero image takes 100vh with text anchored bottom-left.
- **Signature Elements**: Large pull-quotes from fan testimonials; concert ticket-style tour date cards; vinyl record motif in section dividers
- **Interaction Philosophy**: Smooth, warm transitions — like the crowd swaying. Hover reveals feel like turning a page.
- **Animation**: Fade-up entrances with slight blur, parallax on concert photos, smooth scroll-triggered reveals
- **Typography System**: Playfair Display (headlines) + DM Sans (body) — editorial gravitas meets modern clarity

## Selected Approach: **Idea 1 — "Glasgow Brutalist Punk"**

This approach best captures Callum Beattie's raw, authentic Scottish identity and the energy of his live performances. The electric lime accent on near-black creates maximum visual impact and urgency — perfect for driving follow CTAs. The bold typography and asymmetric layout feel like a gig poster come to life.
